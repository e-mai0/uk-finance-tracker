# Cyclops Launch Reels — Final Production Package

**Process:** 7 concept versions → 3 scoring rounds × 5 persona agents (CFO, core-audience
student, doomscrolling founder, competitor head-of-growth, senior creative director) →
every version revised between rounds (one concept killed and replaced) → Round 3
reached unanimous sign-off on 6 of 7. The five below topped the aggregated rankings
**with 5/5 agent sign-off each**. Scripts live in `iterations/round-03.md`; full
scoring history below.

**Format:** Instagram Reels, 9:16, 15s, sound-on designed / sound-off legible.
**Set rules (agent consensus):** one tagline everywhere — *"Cyclops. Never say anything
twice."*; identical end card (eye blink → wordmark → tagline); every video ends on a
human finger clicking Submit; hero stories rotate (V1 rowing · V3 hackathon · V7
securities module) so the set reads as many users, not one demo.

## THE FIVE WINNERS — shooting scripts + render prompts

Render notes that apply to all: AI video models render *exact* UI text unreliably —
for V2 (pure motion graphics) and any close-up of real product UI, the highest-fidelity
path is compositing real Trackr/Cyclops screen captures (or a Remotion build) over
AI-generated live-action plates. Prompts below are written for **Kling 3.0**
(9:16, 15s, sound on, ~30 credits each) and work for Marketing Studio too.

### 🥇 V3 — "Why Goldman?"
*Why it won: proves the hardest claim — one true story, genuinely different arguments
per firm — visually, in one beat. Competitor: "we have no answer to this."*

Script: see `iterations/round-03.md` §V3. Final shoot notes from Round 3: caption
"Over thirty times" (not "Thirty times" after a "31" card); hold each of the two
morphs a full beat; Goldman draft first, Citadel second.

Generation prompt:
> Vertical 9:16 Instagram reel, 15s. A university student at a laptop in a dim room,
> close-up over-the-shoulder: they type "Why Goldman Sachs?" — caption overlay
> "Application 1." Smash cuts, identical framing, the student progressively more
> dead-eyed, typing "Why J.P. Morgan?" "Why Barclays?" "Why Citadel?" with captions
> "Application 9… 17… 31", speed-ramping to a blur. Record scratch, hard cut to black,
> bold white caption: "You've answered this. Over thirty times." Then a clean chat UI:
> the student types one story once; a single elegant drafted sentence appears and
> visibly restructures as the form header flips from one bank to another, caption
> "Same true story. Different argument." A human hand clicks a Submit button, caption
> "It drafts. You decide." End card on ivory background: a claret eye icon blinks,
> wordmark "Cyclops", tagline "Never say anything twice." Energetic beat-synced
> editing, UK student aesthetic, premium minimal UI in ivory and claret.

### 🥈 V7 — "It Doesn't Sound Like a Robot"
*Why it won: kills the #1 conversion objection (AI slop gets you binned); the
twitching-letters rewrite is the set's most shareable single image.*

Script: `iterations/round-03.md` §V7. Shoot notes: two buzzer gags only; rewrite
target line may break across two lines on screen, don't shorten it.

Generation prompt:
> Vertical 9:16 Instagram reel, 15s. Extreme close-up of a cover letter being typed
> on screen: "I am excited to apply…" — a giant red game-show buzzer SLAMS down with
> a harsh BZZT, the words knocked askew; caption "Recruiters can smell this." One more
> cliché types: "…leverage my skills in a fast-paced environment…" — BZZT again,
> letters scattered. Then a long mesmerising transformation: the broken cliché letters
> reflow and rewrite themselves, still twitching as they move, into one human sentence:
> "I took the securities module everyone avoids, and it rewired how I read a balance
> sheet." A thin sidebar strip shows its source: a memory file labelled "coursework:
> securities — first." A human hand changes one word, then clicks Submit; caption
> "Your stories. Your voice. Your click." End card: claret eye blink, "Cyclops.
> Never say anything twice." Punchy sound design, kinetic typography, ivory and
> claret palette.

### 🥉 V4 — "The Speedrun"
*Why it won: most format-native concept (student + founder both ranked it #1);
turns the compliance requirement — the human review — into the joke.*

Script: `iterations/round-03.md` §V4. Shoot notes: timer running with first green
split inside frame 1; timer visibly accumulates the review time into the 03:41 PB.

Generation prompt:
> Vertical 9:16 Instagram reel, 15s, styled as a video-game speedrun. A glowing
> speedrun timer and a vertical splits rail overlay a laptop screen recording of a
> job application form; the timer is already running in frame 1 and the first split
> ticks green instantly: "Personal — 0.3s", then "Education — 0.6s", "Work auth —
> 0.8s" as form fields cascade-fill. Caption: "Internship application — any%."
> Suddenly the split "Why this firm?" appears and the timer dramatically CRAWLS to
> near-stillness; a thoughtful drafted paragraph sits on screen while a student reads
> it and edits one word; the split rail reads "your review — 1:32 and counting."
> Caption over the crawling timer: "The only slow part is you. That's the point."
> The student clicks Submit; the timer slams to "03:41 — PB", with one glance at a
> leaderboard reading "Yesterday (manual): 49:12." End card: claret eye blink,
> "Cyclops. Never say anything twice." Retro-gaming HUD meets premium fintech UI,
> beat-synced, energetic.

### V1 — "2:47 AM"
*Why it won: strongest emotional hook for the exact audience; the v14 filename does
half the work; cleanest pain→relief arc.*

Script: `iterations/round-03.md` §V1. Shoot notes: clock-wipe to 9:00am at the
daylight transition; memory page holds a full 2s.

Generation prompt:
> Vertical 9:16 Instagram reel, 15s, cinematic UGC style. Night: a student's face lit
> only by laptop glow in a dark bedroom, 2:47am on the screen clock; a file save
> dialog types out "goldman_cover_FINAL_v14.docx". Cut to a browser tab bar
> overflowing with fourteen tabs. Tired voiceover: "Fourteen cover letters. Same
> stories. Every time." Clock-wipe to warm daylight: the same student, calm; on
> screen a tidy "memory" page listing their real stories (rowing — captain · hackathon
> · securities module) holds for two seconds, then one tap on a button labelled
> "Apply with Cyclops" and the form fills field by field. Freeze on one large readable
> drafted line: "As captain of the second VIII, I learned what it costs to keep eight
> exhausted people moving in time." — caption "From your memory: rowing — captain."
> The student exhales, mutters "done", and clicks Submit; the laptop closes. End card:
> claret eye blink, "Cyclops. Never say anything twice." Warm, real, no smiling-at-
> laptop ad cliché.

### V2 — "Watch It Count"
*Why it won: best pure mechanic — the pinned counter is "endlessly thumbnail-able",
and "The 1 is your click" is the set's best closing line.*

Script: `iterations/round-03.md` §V2. Shoot notes: don't pause the tick on 41; give
the climb a visible peak so the spin-down has known distance. Strong candidate for a
Remotion/real-UI build instead of generative video (pure motion graphics + one hand
insert).

Generation prompt:
> Vertical 9:16 Instagram reel, 15s, clean minimal motion graphics on a white
> background with claret-red accents. Frame 1: a bold label "Times you'll type 'Why
> this firm?':" with a huge monospaced counter ticking rapidly upward past 41,
> caption "…and counting." — the counter stays pinned in the top corner the whole
> video. Identical question cards land in a strict beat-synced grid. A claret cursor
> answers ONE card; all duplicate cards collapse with a whoosh into a single glowing
> file, caption ON the collapse: "Answer once." The pinned counter spins DOWN as
> three application forms fill one after another, the unfocused ones showing chips
> "awaiting your review." The counter slams to "1", caption "The 1 is your click.",
> and a real human finger clicks Submit. End card: claret eye blink, "Cyclops. Never
> say anything twice." Beat-synced cuts every second, satisfying UI animation,
> premium SaaS launch aesthetic.
