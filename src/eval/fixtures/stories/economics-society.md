## Raw notes
I co-organised a panel event for the Economics Society — brought in three alumni working in buy-side research, fixed income, and consulting. Four of us on the committee, split by responsibility. I took ownership of speaker liaison and the Q&A format. One of the speakers dropped out five days before the event. I had three days to find a replacement. Called everyone I knew, eventually got a third-year PhD student who'd worked at Schroders the previous summer. She was actually better than the original speaker — more recent, more honest about the day-to-day. Event had 120 attendees, which was a record for the society. The bit I'm most proud of is that we ran the Q&A in a proper structured way — pre-submitted questions, mixed with live ones — so it didn't become a networking session where two people dominate the whole hour.

## Final versions
